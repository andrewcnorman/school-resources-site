2012–2013

In 2012–2013, we used 1804.5 kWh of electricity (4.94 kWh/day), 5174.4 kWh of gas (14.2 kWh/day) and 53981 litres of water (147.9 l/day).

Aims:

  • To beat energy certificate for house
  • To beat consumption figures for local area
  • To beat national consumption figures

Methods

  • Most energy efficient white goods possible
  • Replaced lighting with CFL or better
  • Monitoring energy use via meters approximately once per week
  • Thermostat set to low values
  • Thermostat moved to main room from near (draughty) front door
  • Cool bag used for (once weekly) shop cold items to reduce fridge load
  • Energy efficient TV

Lighting

One of the first priorities, after moving in, was to replace horribly inefficient incandescent bulbs (in some cases, 100W bulbs) with more energy efficient lighting solutions. At the time, it was not feasible financially to convert to all LEDs (apart from a couple of cheap bulbs), but hopefully it will be more manageable to convert to LED bulbs as new bulbs are bought in the future or for replacement.

Heating

The house is an end-terrace, so it benefits from having a whole side shared with the neighbours, and it is heated by central heating powered by a condensing gas boiler (mainly) and also a gas fire which is used occasionally in winter.

There is a main computerized thermostat which contols the boiler and central heating via a radio link, and the radiators have individual valves to control their relative outputs when the central heating is on. The thermostat can be programmed to follow a pattern of settings throughout the day, with up to five levels of temperature.
