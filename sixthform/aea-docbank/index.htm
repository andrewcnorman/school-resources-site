AEA Physics

The Advanced Extension Awards (AEAs) were introduced in 2002, as a means of testing students at the most demanding standards found across the world. They were aimed at the top 10% of A-level students, taken in the final year of school.

They provided a way for universities to distiguish the very best candidates nationally (in 2008, 25.8% of A-level entries were given an A grade). With the introduction of the A* grade at A-level, they were deemed no longer necessary, and were withdrawn after June 2009.
